Badass (Archaeological spatial and attribute database) is a spatial database developed by Caroline Font, Thomas Guillemard, Florent Mercey and Christelle Seng of the Institut national de recherches archéologiques préventives (Inrap). This database is based, on the one hand, on the institutional structuring of the "Six Layers" of spatial data from topographic surveys (developed at Inrap by the network of GIS referents led by Anne Moreau), and on the other hand, on a classic archaeological record based on stratigraphy (Galinié, Randoin 1987).

This extension makes it possible to generate two files:
- the spatial database file in SpatiaLite format (.sqlite)
- the QGIS project (.qgz) which integrates the tables, relations and styles which allow the use of interfaces for entering attribute data in the form of forms
Two extensions are under development:
- Badass of the Dead: allows the addition of tables, thesauri and a dummy for the entry and study of anthropological data
- House of Badass: allows the addition of tables and thesauri for the capture and study of archaeological data on the building
The programming of this extension was carried out by Alexandre Humeau, Emma Chapuis and Alex Baiet.
